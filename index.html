Research Focus

My work lies at the interface of high-dimensional theory and data-driven applications. I approach these problems with a balance of rigorous mathematical analysis and collaborative field work.

High-dimensional Inference

Developing adaptive procedures for selective inference and uncertainty quantification when the number of parameters dwarfs the sample size.

  • Sparsity-aware confidence sets
  • Post-selection validity guarantees

Change-point Analysis

Designing nonparametric methods to detect abrupt distributional shifts in dependent data streams with provable delay and false alarm control.

  • Sequential monitoring
  • Bootstrap calibration

Statistical Learning Theory

Bridging empirical process techniques with modern machine learning models to understand generalisation in structured prediction tasks.

  • Complexity regularisation
  • Robust representation learning

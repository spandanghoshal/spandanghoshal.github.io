Research

Preprints, ongoing projects,
and areas of inquiry.

I am broadly interested in nonparametric inference and sequential methods for dependent data — procedures that remain robust under weak dependence and heavy tails.

Focus

Current directions

§ i

Change-Point Detection

Nonparametric, distance-based methods for identifying distributional shifts in multivariate and high-dimensional data streams, with control over detection delay and false alarms.

Pairwise distancesBootstrap calibration
§ ii

Distribution-Free Testing

Multivariate two-sample problems approached through combinatorial and graph-based statistics — notably runs tests built on the shortest Hamiltonian path.

Runs testsConsistency theory
§ iii

High-Dimensional Statistics

Asymptotic behaviour of inference procedures when the dimension grows with — or far exceeds — the sample size, including HDLSS regimes.

HDLSS asymptoticsHeavy tails
§ iv

Random Graphs & Motifs

Estimation of motif counts and structural functionals in large random graphs, and the statistical properties of such estimators under different sampling models.

Motif estimationNetwork models

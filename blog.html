Mathematical Meditations

September 29, 2024 ¬∑ Research Reflections

The past few weeks have been a deep dive into a paper on change-point detection under dependent noise. The mathematics is intricate, and it is easy to lose clarity when proofs span several pages. What kept me grounded was a morning routine of chanting, pranayama, and journaling the key intuitions behind each lemma before touching the keyboard. This ritual not only improved my focus but also helped me communicate the ideas more gracefully to collaborators.

In the post, I outline the steps of this routine, share a few diagrams from my notebook, and reflect on how a mindful approach can turn mathematical frustration into a meditative practice.

Change-Points in the Wild

July 07, 2024 ¬∑ Applied Statistics

A collaboration with a group of ecologists introduced me to a river in Assam whose health indicators had been monitored for over a decade. Sudden spikes in nutrient levels hinted at anthropogenic interference, and we needed a robust method to quantify the shifts. We deployed a nonparametric change-point algorithm that could handle missing data and seasonal effects.

The project reminded me of the importance of communicating uncertainty with humility. In the post I discuss the modelling choices we made, how we involved local stakeholders in interpreting the results, and why statistics can serve as a bridge between communities and policy makers.
